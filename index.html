Background & Focus

I am a Risk Analysis and Diagnostics Engineer at Enbridge, where I develop and apply advanced integrity and risk assessment frameworks for pipeline systems and energy facilities. My role involves evaluating complex failure mechanisms, identifying critical risk scenarios, and translating engineering insight into actionable mitigation strategies.

My technical foundation combines solid mechanics, fracture and fatigue analysis, finite element modelling, and data-driven approaches — applied together to enhance the reliability and safety of high-consequence energy infrastructure.

I hold an MSc in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Alberta (GPA 3.9) and a BSc from K.N. Toosi University of Technology, and I am registered as an Engineer-in-Training (E.I.T.) with APEGA.

Core Technical Focus

  • Pipeline integrity analysis and fitness-for-service evaluation (ASME B31, API-579)
  • XFEM-based fracture modelling and fatigue life prediction
  • Static, dynamic, and vibration FEA using Abaqus and ANSYS
  • Risk-informed decision making and reliability modelling
  • Structural health monitoring with applied machine learning
  • Design for manufacturability (DFM) and root cause analysis
