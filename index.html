Background & Focus

Research and professional work spanning energy infrastructure, structural integrity, mechanical design, and applied machine learning.

I am a Risk Analysis and Diagnostics Engineer at Enbridge, developing advanced integrity and risk assessment frameworks for pipeline systems and energy facilities. My role involves evaluating complex failure mechanisms, identifying critical risk scenarios, and translating engineering insight into actionable mitigation strategies.

My technical foundation combines solid mechanics, fracture and fatigue analysis, finite element modelling, and data-driven approaches to enhance the reliability of high-consequence energy infrastructure.

I hold an MSc in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Alberta and a BSc from K.N. Toosi University of Technology. I am registered as an Engineer-in-Training (E.I.T.) with APEGA.

Core Technical Focus

  • Pipeline integrity analysis and fitness-for-service evaluation
  • Fracture mechanics analysis and fatigue life prediction
  • Static, dynamic, and vibration FEA — Abaqus & ANSYS
  • Risk-informed decision making and reliability modelling
  • Applied data analysis and machine learning
  • Design for manufacturability (DFM) and root cause analysis
